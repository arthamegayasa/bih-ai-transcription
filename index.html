Today at BIH, SOAP notes flow into TrakCare through a mix of typing during the visit, paper drafts, and after-hours catch-up. The cost is invisible on a balance sheet — but it shows up as fatigue, late finishes, and brief consultations. AI medical transcription promises to give some of those hours back.

Why this is not a generic IT decision

Healthcare data in Indonesia is special — by law and by accreditation.

Legal

UU PDP (UU 27/2022)

Health information is classified as data pribadi spesifik — sensitive personal data. Cross-border transfer requires demonstrable adequacy and explicit consent.

Regulatory

PP 28/2024

Operationalizes obligations on Penyelenggara Sistem Elektronik — including DPIA, breach notification, and audit trails for personal data processing.

Accreditation

JCI IM Standards

As a JCI hospital, BIH must demonstrate continuous control over patient health information — including who accesses it, where it lives, and how it leaves the institution.

The point: we don't have to choose between compliant and fast. We can architect for both — but only if data residency is a first-class design decision, not an afterthought negotiated with a vendor.

Before We Decide

Ten questions to answer first.

Whether we buy or build, the same questions apply.

Privacy & Compliance

  1. Where do audio and transcripts physically reside at every step?
  2. Has a DPIA been completed under UU PDP / PP 28/2024?
  3. Can our data be used to train the vendor's model? (Should be no.)
  4. What is the retention policy for raw audio?

Clinical Safety

  1. Who signs off on accuracy? AI never auto-writes to TrakCare.
  2. How are sensitive cases (psychiatry, HIV, medikolegal) flagged?
  3. What is the fail-safe when the AI is unavailable?

Operational

  1. TrakCare integration — HL7/FHIR or custom?
  2. Total cost of ownership over 3 years, not year 1.
  3. Exit strategy — can we leave with our data and our templates?
